2. The Service

TrainClear provides predictions and estimates about whether railroad crossings in the Portland, Oregon metropolitan area are, or may soon be, blocked by train traffic. Predictions are generated by aggregating publicly available data sources (described in Section 4) with machine-learning and rule-based models, supplemented by voluntary user reports.

  • Coverage is limited to specific crossings in the Portland metropolitan area. The Service has no information about crossings outside its published coverage list.
  • The Service is currently offered in beta. Accuracy, availability, and features may change without notice.
  • Predictions are displayed with confidence qualifiers (for example HIGH, MEDIUM, or LOW). No prediction — at any confidence level — is a statement of fact about current or future conditions at a crossing.

4. Data Sources and Lawful Collection

TrainClear generates predictions exclusively from lawful, publicly available, or commercially licensed sources:

  • Publicly broadcast railroad radio. We receive and process unencrypted radio transmissions (such as trackside defect-detector broadcasts and road channel traffic) that are broadcast in the clear on public airwaves and are readily accessible to the general public. Receiving such transmissions is lawful under federal law (see 18 U.S.C. § 2511(2)(g)(i)). We do not decrypt, descramble, intercept private communications, or access any railroad computer system, network, or non-public communication channel.
  • Public traffic cameras. We process imagery from publicly available ODOT and city traffic cameras that anyone can view.
  • Public transit and rail feeds. Amtrak's public train-location API and TriMet's public GTFS-RT feeds.
  • Licensed commercial traffic data. Road congestion and flow data from commercial providers under license.
  • Voluntary user reports submitted through the app.

TrainClear is an independent product. We are not affiliated with, endorsed by, or connected to Union Pacific Railroad, BNSF Railway, Portland Terminal Railroad, Amtrak, TriMet, ODOT, or any railroad or government agency.
