The Fusion Engine

Six live signals. One straight answer.

No trackside hardware. TrainClear cross-checks independent public feeds, so one signal confirms another — and one camera hit can update an entire corridor of crossings at once.

in ~8 min · 5–8 min block

Railroad radio

Defect detectors and crew traffic on UP and BNSF road channels, transcribed in real time with speech-to-text. Often the earliest signal there is.

ODOT traffic cameras

Computer vision watches public traffic cameras near the crossings 24/7 and confirms trains — and gate states — the moment they're visible.

Amtrak GPS

Live locations for the Cascades and Coast Starlight, with per-crossing ETAs computed from actual speed — like the app's "Amtrak GPS verified" badge.

TriMet GTFS-RT

MAX Orange Line movements at the at-grade crossings it shares with freight — 67 transits a day through the SE Inner Cluster alone.

Traffic congestion

Sudden flow anomalies on the streets feeding a crossing corroborate a blockage even when no camera has line-of-sight.

Bus detours

When TriMet buses suddenly detour around a crossing, that's evidence. TrainClear uses it to corroborate what the other signals are saying.

Corridor propagation: crossings are grouped the way the track actually runs — SE Inner Cluster, SE Hawthorne Corridor, NW Corridor and more. One confirmed sighting projects along the corridor, so all six SE Hawthorne crossings update from a single camera hit.
