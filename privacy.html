Privacy Policy

Effective: July 14, 2026 · Last updated: July 14, 2026

Plain-English summary (the full policy below governs): We collect as little as we can. Your location is used on your device to find nearby crossings — we don't store it. We keep an anonymous device token so we can send you push alerts, basic anonymous analytics so we can fix bugs, and your email only if you give it to us for the beta or referrals. We never sell personal information, and we never see your payment card.

1. What We Collect

1.1 Location (on-demand, not stored)

When you open the app, we request your device's location to determine which crossings are nearest to you and to center the map. Location is used in real time; we do not store your precise location on our servers or build any history of where you have been. If you deny location permission, the app still works — you can select crossings manually.

1.2 Push notification tokens

To deliver blockage alerts, we store the anonymous push token that Apple (APNs) or Google (FCM) assigns to your device, together with your alert preferences (which crossings, quiet hours, lead time). The token identifies your device to the notification service; it does not contain your name, number, or location.

1.3 Crossing reports

If you submit a crossing report ("train present" / "train cleared"), we collect the crossing identifier, report type, timestamp, and an anonymous device identifier used for abuse prevention. Reports are not linked to your name or email.

1.4 Subscription status

If you subscribe to Pro, we receive a purchase-confirmation token from Apple or Google to verify your entitlement. We never receive or store your card number, billing address, or bank details — Apple and Google process all payments under their own privacy policies.

1.5 Analytics and crash data

We use Google Firebase to collect anonymous, aggregated usage data: app opens, feature usage, device model and OS version, crash reports, and coarse (city-level) region. This is not linked to your identity.

1.6 Email and referral data (only if you give it)

If you join the beta waitlist on our website or in the app, we collect the name, email, and role (e.g., commuter, cyclist) you provide, and use them to send your invite and service updates. If you participate in the referral program, we store the linkage between your referral code and signups it produced, to credit your free months.

2. What We Do NOT Collect

  • No continuous or background GPS tracking, and no location history.
  • No name, email, or phone number is required to use the free app.
  • No payment card data — ever. The app stores handle all billing.
  • No contacts, photos, microphone audio, or camera data from your device.
  • No advertising identifiers, no third-party ad SDKs, no cross-app tracking.
  • We do not sell personal information, and we do not share it for targeted advertising.

4. Public Data Sources We Process

TrainClear's predictions are built from public and licensed feeds that do not involve your personal data:

  • Publicly broadcast railroad radio (unencrypted defect-detector and road-channel audio). No user data is sent to or received from these sources.
  • Public ODOT/city traffic cameras. These cameras show public roadways and may incidentally capture vehicles or pedestrians in public view. We process this imagery automatically to detect trains and gate states; we do not use it to identify individuals, read plates, or track anyone.
  • Amtrak public train-location API and TriMet public GTFS-RT feeds. No user data is exchanged.
  • Licensed commercial traffic-flow data. Aggregate road-speed data; no TrainClear user data is provided to these vendors.

5. Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell personal information. We share information only:

  • With service providers that process data on our behalf (e.g., Google Firebase for notifications, analytics, and crash reporting; cloud hosting providers), bound to use it only for our purposes.
  • As aggregated, anonymized data. We may share aggregated crossing and prediction statistics (never personal information) with partners such as emergency services or logistics companies, including through a B2B API. This data cannot reasonably be used to identify you.
  • For legal reasons, if required by law, regulation, legal process, or enforceable governmental request, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of any person.
  • In a business transfer (merger, acquisition, or asset sale), in which case this policy continues to apply to previously collected data.

7. Your Rights and Choices

7.1 Everyone

Regardless of where you live, you can email privacy@trainclear.com to ask what data we hold associated with your device or email, request correction or deletion, or opt out of non-essential emails (every marketing email also has an unsubscribe link). You can disable push notifications and location access at any time in your device settings.

7.2 Oregon residents (OCPA)

Under the Oregon Consumer Privacy Act you may have the right to access, correct, delete, and obtain a copy of personal data, and to opt out of targeted advertising, sale, or significant profiling. We do not sell personal data or use it for targeted advertising or such profiling; we will honor all OCPA rights that apply. If we decline a request, you may appeal by replying to our decision, and we will respond within the statutory period; unresolved appeals may be referred to the Oregon Attorney General.

7.3 California residents (CCPA/CPRA)

You have the right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of sale/sharing of personal information, and to non-discrimination for exercising these rights. We do not sell or share personal information as defined by the CCPA. Submit requests to privacy@trainclear.com; we will verify requests using the email or device identifier associated with your data.

7.4 Global Privacy Control

Our website does not use cross-site tracking, so there is nothing for a GPC signal to opt you out of — but we honor the intent of such signals.

8. Children's Privacy

The Service is intended for users 16 and older and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13; if we learn that we have, we will delete it promptly. Parents or guardians may contact privacy@trainclear.com.

9. Security

We use commercially reasonable technical and organizational safeguards — encrypted transport (TLS), access controls, and data minimization (the best safeguard is the data we never collect). No system is perfectly secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security. The public feeds we consume are already public; we add no personal data to them.

10. Where Data Is Processed

TrainClear operates from the United States, and the Service is offered for use in the Portland, Oregon area. Data is processed on servers in the United States and the European Union (hosting providers). If you use the Service from outside the U.S., you understand your data will be processed in these locations.

11. Changes to This Policy

We may update this policy from time to time. Material changes will be announced in the app or by other reasonable means, and the "Last updated" date above will change. Continued use after changes take effect constitutes acceptance.
